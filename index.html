About

I am an Assistant Professor in the Department of Biochemistry and Molecular Genetics at Midwestern University in Downers Grove, IL. My scientific career has been driven by a fascination for transcription factors as biological tools to change a cell type into another cell type, an approach called cellular reprogramming.

jlerne@midwestern.edu · Academic profile · Curriculum Vitae · Google Scholar · ORCID
Research

The core objective of my lab is to enhance cell reprogramming for future clinical applications in regenerative medicine and tissue rejuvenation. I have a particular focus on generating induced Pluripotent Stem Cells (iPSCs), liver tissue and neurons.

One of the main safeguards impeding reprogramming is heterochromatin, which embeds reprogramming-inducing genes into a hypercompact, inaccessible and repressed chromatin structure. By investigating the interplay between transcription factors and heterochromatin, my lab aims to reveal novel pathways to overcome the heterochromatin barrier and improve the efficiency of cell reprogramming.

My favorite research tools are live-cell imaging and genomics.
